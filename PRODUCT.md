# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

High-net-worth individuals, family offices, institutional partners, and prospective co-investors evaluating Grupo Franklin. They arrive with existing context — through a referral or prior relationship — and use the site to confirm legitimacy, not to be convinced. They are sophisticated and have seen many firm websites.

## Product Purpose

Digital presence for a private investment firm. The site communicates that the firm exists, is serious, and can be reached. It is not a conversion funnel. Success looks like: a visitor arrives, feels the firm is real and credible, finds the contact information, and leaves with a clear impression of the firm's character.

## Brand Personality

Discreet. Serious. Established. Old-money restraint — confidence expressed through understatement, not assertion. Nothing is decorated that doesn't need to be. The firm doesn't explain itself.

## Anti-references

Generic startup fintech (Brex, Ramp, Robinhood-era "we're disrupting finance" energy): smooth hero gradients, san-serif taglines about transformation, feature-card grids, high-saturation call-to-action buttons. This firm predates the startup wave and has nothing to prove.

## Design Principles

1. **Restraint is authority.** Every element earns its place. Decoration that isn't doing structural work comes out.
2. **Specificity over assertion.** The site states facts; it does not make claims about its own quality. Copy says what the firm is, not how great it is.
3. **Timeless over current.** Aesthetic choices should feel appropriate in five years. Avoid trends. Avoid anything that will look dated.
4. **Confidence without performance.** Motion, if present, is composed and unhurried. Nothing bounces, springs, or demands attention.
5. **Legibility is non-negotiable.** Every piece of text passes contrast. Elegance through illegibility is not elegance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All text ≥4.5:1 contrast. Focus states visible and clear. Reduced-motion alternative for all animations.
